    • About Jonathan

    Jonathan Higger is a Software Developer with a background in Chemical Engineering looking to apply creativity and work ethic towards building well designed web or computer applications.

    Schooling

    From 2011 to 2018, I attended Florida State University where I went from being a pre-med student to being a Chemical Engineering graduate. I remember being in the engineering school from 7am to 9pm almost every day from my junior year to when I graduated. But it wasn't until after graduating that I realized that I didn't like Engineering as much as I thought I would, and that while I appreciated the challenge and loved learning, I really missed coding like I had done in school.

    Finally, after two years of working as a construction estimator, I decided to enroll at Flatiron Denver to learn how to become a software engineer. There I felt that the skills I learned prepared me for a lifelong journey and pursuit of technological knowledge.

    Employment

    After college, I moved straight to Colorado from Florida, knowing full well that I'm the type to take a painful hike over a relaxing day at the beach. At first the job hunt was tough, but after a couple of hard-working months I landed a job as an estimator for powerline construction company called Great Southwestern construction. What I loved about the job was that it gave me the opportunity to learn about the construction industry, the bidding process of high dollar government jobs, and most importantly practical coding. While I worked there, I was able to occasionally solve company problems using Visual Basic.

    Hobbies

    When I am not coding, or learning about software, I really enjoy making music. In College, I founded an Audio Production club that is still running to this day, I produced for a local Tallahassee record label, and even performed in a band. I play guitar, bass, and piano and listen to all types of music. As well as music, I try to get out and exercise by mountainbiking, hiking, and rock climbing. After all, I did move out to Colorado for a reason!.
